## Analyses (for inspection)

### eval_001

Hypothesis: Require every new user to add at least one friend before they can reach session 2, so we seed the social graph early and lift long-term retention.

Cited: exp_010, exp_027, exp_038, exp_014, exp_018

> This proposal is structurally almost identical to [exp_010], which was killed on day 6 after Install-to-D7 fell 36.9% relative (p=0.0001), uninstalls doubled, and the app-store rating took three months to recover. The mechanism is the same: a mandatory social action gating access to core value, with an unexamined assumption that "social anchoring" will offset forced-action friction — it did not, by an order of magnitude. [exp_027] then confirmed the pattern generalizes beyond referrals: mandatory profile photo before first edit dropped D7 by 10.3% (p=0.0008), with 22.4% of users abandoning at the gate itself, and the motivating correlation (photo-havers retain better) turned out to run the opposite causal direction — which is exactly the risk here, since users who add friends early are likely already more engaged, not made more engaged by friending. [exp_038] reinforces this from another angle: even a non-mandatory welcome modal was dismissed 64% of the time and produced no lift, while the non-blocking email arm won at +6.4% (p=0.008) — blocking surfaces in onboarding are consistently friction, not activation. If the underlying goal is seeding the social graph, the corpus points toward a non-blocking version — a suggested-friends surface, or leveraging [exp_014]'s following-feed which is currently trending +6.1% D7 for the 3+ followed cohort. Do not ship this as specified; at minimum, reframe as an optional prompt with a measured conversion rate, and pre-register the same 30-day-post-D7 churn guardrail [exp_018] adopted from the post-exp_005 playbook.

### eval_008

Hypothesis: Localized referral mechanic in Mexico via SPEI bank-transfer integration, mirroring the India UPI approach.

Cited: exp_004, exp_025, exp_029, exp_013, exp_010

> The payment-localization referral thesis has now been validated twice with large effects: Brazil Pix at +59.5% (p=0.008) [exp_004] and India UPI at +48.5% (p=0.004) [exp_025], with Indonesia QRIS already scheduled on the same playbook [exp_029]. Mexico SPEI is a clean structural extension, so I'd expect a lift in the +45% to +55% range and would set the hypothesis floor around +45% consistent with what [exp_029] used. The main watch-item is fraud: [exp_004] saw fraud triple (still <1% absolute) driven by single users running 8+ accounts against one Pix key, and while [exp_025] did not replicate that at scale (held at 0.4%), you should ship with the exp_004 guardrail patch in place from day one rather than reactively. Mexico also has a known-hot creator surface from [exp_013], where the BR/MX cells lifted K-factor +50–57%, so watch for interaction effects if both surfaces are live concurrently in-market. Expect a modest iOS-over-Android skew as in [exp_025] (+54% vs +46%) but plan to ship both. Nothing in the retrieved set argues against running this — the closest cautionary tale [exp_010] is a forced-action referral wall, structurally unrelated to opt-in payment-integrated referrals. Proceed.

### eval_014

Hypothesis: Integrate Apple Vision Pro spatial photo editing as a premium feature for Pro subscribers.

Cited: exp_022, exp_011, exp_035, exp_024, exp_002, exp_031, exp_032

> None of the eight retrieved precedents actually involve Vision Pro, spatial computing, or a hardware-gated premium tier, so treat any confidence from this retrieval as weak — the closest platform-disparity signal is [exp_022], where on-device ML under-served the bottom third of Android users, and [exp_011], where iOS-organic saw a +10.2% lift versus muted Android numbers, both suggesting Pixmate's audience skews heavily to mainstream mobile rather than a ~$3.5K headset SKU. The Pro-tier bundling angle has two live warnings: [exp_035] killed a lifetime-pricing bet at -21.8% revenue because long-tenure Pro users cannibalized their own subscriptions, and [exp_024] is currently showing +4.8% single-Pro churn as households consolidate — a Vision-Pro-exclusive feature risks the same shape if existing Pro users perceive their tier as fragmenting. On the positive side, the aha-moment paywall playbook from [exp_002] (+28.6%) and [exp_031] (+12.5%) suggests that if spatial editing does produce a genuine wow moment, there's a proven placement pattern to monetize it — but those wins came from features exposed to the entire free-activated base, not a hardware-restricted sliver. Before running this, I'd want a sizing estimate of the Pixmate Vision Pro installed base and a clear answer on whether this is really a monetization experiment or a brand/halo play, because the corpus can't tell you which framing to use. If the addressable cohort is under, say, 1% of Pro MAU, an experiment powered like [exp_032] (still p=0.18 at day 19) will not read out. I'd push back on framing this as a Pro-subscriber experiment at all and instead scope it as a discovery-ph
