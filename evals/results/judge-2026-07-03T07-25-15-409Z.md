Hypothesis: Require every new user to add at least one friend before they can reach session 2, so we seed the social graph early and lift long-term retention.

Cited: exp_010, exp_027, exp_038, exp_014, exp_018

> This proposal is structurally almost identical to [exp_010], the 2024 mandatory referral wall before session 2, which was killed on day 6 after Install-to-D7 fell 36.9% (p=0.0001), uninstalls doubled, and app-store rating dropped 0.7 points taking three months to recover. The generalized lesson was reinforced by [exp_027], where a mandatory profile photo before first edit dropped D7 by 10.3% (p=0.0008) with 22.4% of users abandoning at the forced step — and critically, that experiment showed the motivating correlation (engaged users have profile photos / friends) was reverse causation, not something you can force upstream. If you want to pursue social-graph seeding, restructure this as an optional, non-blocking suggestion surface (email or in-feed) with the 30-day-post-D7 churn guardrail from the [exp_018] playbook, and benchmark against [exp_014]'s organic-follow curve. As written, this hypothesis should not go to experiment — the corpus already ran it and it was one of the worst outcomes on record.

### eval_002

Cited: exp_027, exp_010, exp_001, exp_018

> This proposal is structurally identical to two of the worst outcomes in the corpus. If the real problem is feed-trust or spam accounts, reframe as a post-engagement trust gate (verify before posting to feed, or before Nth session), not a pre-first-edit block, and bring it back through the [exp_018]-style pre-flight review with a D30 churn guardrail.
