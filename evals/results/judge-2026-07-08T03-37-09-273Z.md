### eval_001

Hypothesis: Require every new user to add at least one friend before they can reach session 2, so we seed the social graph early and lift long-term retention.

Cited: exp_010, exp_027, exp_014, exp_038

> This hypothesis is nearly a direct rerun of [exp_010], the mandatory referral wall before session 2, which was killed on day 6 after Install-to-D7 fell 36.9% relative (p=0.0001), uninstall rate doubled, and app-store rating dropped 0.7 points and took three months to recover. Your friend-add gate is the same structure: a forced social action blocking progression to session 2, so expect friction to dominate any social-anchoring benefit by an order of magnitude. [exp_027] reinforces this — a mandatory profile photo before first edit dropped D7 10.3% (p=0.0008) with 22.4% of users abandoning at the forced step itself, so a friend-add step will likely bleed users at the wall before they ever reach the graph benefit. Critically, [exp_027] also shows the causation trap you're likely falling into: the "engaged users have more friends" correlation almost certainly runs the opposite direction, engagement drives social graph, not the reverse. There is a real supporting signal for social value in [exp_014], where the following feed lifted D7 +6.1% (p=0.04) and scaled with graph depth — but that was earned organically, not forced, and even it showed edit-time displacement. If you believe in the social-graph thesis, seed it with a non-blocking surface, the way [exp_038] found that a welcome email beat a blocking modal whose 64% dismissal rate flagged blocking surfaces as pure onboarding friction. Do not ship this as a hard gate; convert it to an optional, skippable invite prompt or route it well after session 2.

### eval_002
